SECTION: WHAT AWAaz SETU IS

Explain with cards:

1. Anonymous Intake Layer
   Users can share their situation without mandatory login, registration, email, or phone number.

2. Evidence-Aware Documentation
   Users may upload screenshots, messages, photos, PDFs, audio, or documents in demo mode. Evidence is helpful but not mandatory.

3. Severity Triage
   Reports are categorized into L1, L2, L3, or L4 based on urgency and risk.

4. Token-Based Follow-Up
   After submission, users receive a mock anonymous token such as AWZ-4821-K.

5. Support Routing
   Depending on the case, the system can guide users toward counselors, NGOs, legal advisors, Cyber Crime Portal, Tele-MANAS, NCW, SHe-Box, district committee, or emergency support.

6. Government Insight
   Anonymous aggregated trends can help district administration understand safety concerns without exposing personal identities.

PAGE 13: POLICY CENTER

Create policy page with tabs or accordion.

1. Privacy Policy
   Content:
   Awaaz Setu collects minimum necessary information. Users can submit reports without mandatory name, email, or phone. Optional contact is collected only if user chooses follow-up support. Reports are not made public.

2. Terms of Use
   Users must submit true and responsible information. Awaaz Setu does not declare guilt and does not replace official authorities.

3. Evidence Handling Policy
   Evidence may include screenshots, messages, photos, audio, video, PDFs, or documents. In this prototype, upload is demo-only. In production, evidence must be encrypted, access-controlled, and handled under approved protocols.

4. False Report & Misuse Policy
   Fake, edited, AI-generated, misleading, malicious, or revenge-based reports are prohibited. Suspicious reports may be reviewed, rejected, or flagged.

5. Child Safety Policy
   Reports involving minors, bad touch, grooming, blackmail, or abuse must be handled with special care and authorized legal/child protection channels.

6. Crisis Support Disclaimer
   Awaaz Setu is not an emergency response service. If someone is in immediate danger, they should contact emergency services, hospital, police, verified helplines, or trusted people nearby.

PAGE 14: FAQ PAGE

Add questions and answers:

Q1. Is Awaaz Setu anonymous?

Q6. Is evidence mandatory?
No. Evidence is helpful but not mandatory.

Q7. Can Awaaz Setu help during emergency?
It can show guidance and helplines, but it is not an emergency response service.

Those are important official/support systems. Awaaz Setu acts as a safe first-step bridge that helps users reach the right system.

Q9. Who will review reports in production?
Only trained and authorized moderators, counselors, legal advisors, NGOs, or district-approved bodies based on role and case type.

Q10. Will victims have to pay?
No. Core reporting should remain free for users. Sustainability can come from grants, CSR, institutional dashboards, and training programs.
